About Spectrum of Grace

Spectrum of Grace was founded with a singular vision: to create a safe, compassionate space where families could heal and grow together. Our founder, Dr. Patricia Vasquez, spent over two decades working in traditional clinical settings before realizing that many families were falling through the cracks—they needed more personalized, accessible, and integrative care. In 2018, she established Spectrum of Grace with a small team of dedicated therapists who shared her passion for family wellness. What began as a boutique practice has grown into a thriving therapeutic community, but we've maintained our core commitment: treating every family member with dignity, respect, and genuine care.

At Spectrum of Grace, our mission is simple yet profound: to empower families to navigate life's challenges with resilience, compassion, and authentic connection. We believe that every family has the capacity for healing and growth when provided with the right support and tools. Our core values—authenticity, evidence-based practice, cultural sensitivity, and unconditional positive regard—guide everything we do. We're committed to creating a diverse, inclusive environment where clients of all backgrounds feel welcomed and understood. We continuously invest in our team's professional development and stay at the forefront of therapeutic innovation to ensure our clients receive the highest quality, most effective care available.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our therapy services and approach.

The duration of family therapy varies depending on your family's unique needs and goals. Some families benefit from short-term therapy (8-12 sessions), while others prefer ongoing support over several months or years. During your initial consultation, we'll discuss your specific situation and recommend a treatment plan that aligns with your objectives. We're flexible and can adjust the frequency and duration of sessions based on your progress and changing needs.

Resistance to therapy is quite common and completely understandable. Our therapists are trained in motivational interviewing and other techniques to gently address resistance without judgment. We often start with individual sessions to build rapport and help resistant family members understand how therapy can benefit them personally. We create a non-threatening environment where everyone's concerns are validated, and we move at a pace that feels comfortable for all participants.

Absolutely. All of our therapists hold current state licensure and advanced credentials in their respective fields. Our team includes Licensed Marriage and Family Therapists (LMFT), Licensed Clinical Counselors (LCC), Licensed Professional Counselors (LPC), and clinical social workers. Additionally, many of our therapists pursue specialized training in areas like trauma-informed care, child psychology, and systemic therapy. We maintain the highest professional standards and require ongoing continuing education to ensure our team remains current with the latest therapeutic research and best practices.

Yes, confidentiality is a cornerstone of our practice. All information shared in therapy sessions is protected by therapist-client privilege and HIPAA regulations. There are limited exceptions where we're legally required to disclose information, such as if there's immediate danger to someone's safety, suspected abuse of children or vulnerable adults, or a court order. We discuss these exceptions during your first session so you understand the boundaries of confidentiality. Your privacy and trust are paramount to us.

Yes, we offer both in-person and virtual therapy sessions to accommodate your preferences and schedule. Virtual sessions are conducted via secure, HIPAA-compliant video conferencing platforms and are just as effective as in-person sessions for many families. We find that virtual therapy can be particularly convenient for busy families, those in remote areas, or situations where transportation is challenging. During your initial consultation, we can discuss which format works best for your family's needs.
